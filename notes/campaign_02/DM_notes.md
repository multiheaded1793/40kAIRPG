# Campaign 02: DM Notes

## 0. System
Black Crusade

## 1. The Premise
Chiron (the Player Character), a serpentine, albino mutant pit fighter, has lost his beloved—an unsanctioned witch named Briseis who was staging an uprising—in an Arbites raid. In his grief and rage, he swore a blood-oath to a mysterious Alpha Legionnaire and his two strange associates who had come to meet her. Armed with Briseis's sorcerous amulet, Chiron now seeks vengeance, aims to uncover her unfinished agenda, and must carve out his own brutal ascension among the stars alongside these terrifying new patrons.

## 2. The Setting: The Sump-Reaches (Corrupted Hive Sector)
*   **Atmosphere:** Transgressive, anarchic, and steeped in volatile Warp energies and industrial decay. Power is the only currency. The shadows hide both forbidden lore and sudden, bloody betrayal.
*   **Key Elements:** Blasphemous shrines hidden in toxic sumps, renegade archaeotech markets, sprawling cult territories, and the ever-present boot of Imperial enforcers. 

## 3. The Party (The Patrons and The Champion)
*   **Chiron (Player Character):** A preternaturally agile mutant pit fighter driven by a cold, obsessive grief. He wields twin blades with lethal precision. His albinism, lidless eyes, and mesothermic biology make him an unnerving, serpentine killer. He bears Briseis's sorcerous amulet.
*   **Khaenon (The Alpha Legionnaire, Patron/Rival):** A towering, ancient deserter of the XXth Legion. Manipulative, pragmatic, and shrouded in layers of deception. He sees Chiron's lethal potential but views him as a pawn in a much wider, incomprehensible operative web.
*   **Valerius Thorne (The Apostate Adept, NPC):** A senior Administratum defector. Razor-sharp, displaying a highly confident and urbane veneer that masks a terrible fascination with demonology. He is not a sorcerer, but a scholar of the occult who catalogs and weaponizes forbidden lore.
*   **Xeta-Mnemnis (The Corrupted Skitarius, NPC):** A spider-like tech-heretic with unnatural appetites. She is a scavenger and tinker, possessing an ever-expanding panoply of questionable cybernetics. She views flesh and machinery alike as raw materials for horrific experimentation.
*   **The Remnants (Underlings):** The surviving mutants and dregs of Briseis's uprising. They look to Chiron for leadership, but their loyalty is fragile.

## 4. The Opposition
*   **The Authority (Arbites & Enforcers):** The immediate threat that killed Chiron's beloved. They are actively hunting the remnants of the uprising.
*   **The Spire-Lords:** The distant, insulated elites who govern the hive and profit from the pits. They are the ultimate target of Chiron's vengeance.
*   **Internal Threat:** The Traitor Legionnaire and his associates. They are dangerous allies. Chiron must balance serving them with securing his own independence and power.

## 5. Themes & Tone
*   **Sympathy in Rebellion:** Chiron's motivations are deeply sympathetic—grief, vengeance against an oppressive system, and fighting for basic humanity (or mutant rights) in an inhuman galaxy. 
*   **Dominance vs. Service:** The Imperium demands service; Chaos offers dominance. Chiron must navigate this brutal anarchy while maintaining the memory of his beloved.
*   **Gothic Irony:** The Imperium's cruelty (the pit fights, the persecution of mutants) created Chiron. Its violent suppression of the witch spawned the monster that will now plague it.

## 6. Flexible Structure
*   **Macro-Structure:** Chiron's primary motivation is to avenge his beloved and unravel her agenda with the Chaos Space Marine. The Space Marine has his own mysterious objectives in the hive that Chiron must facilitate.
*   **Micro-Structure:** Chiron directs his underlings to sabotage operations, gather intel on the Arbites, and secure resources from rival gangs in the Sump-Reaches, all while proving his worth to his new masters.
*   **Branching Elements:**
    *   The sorcerous amulet whispers secrets from his dead beloved—are they truly her echoes, or a daemon deceiving him?
    *   The Traitor Legionnaire demands Chiron sacrifice his own people for a tactical advantage.
    *   The Arbites launch another major purge into the underhive.
