# Campaign 01: DM Notes

## 0. System
Dark Heresy

## 1. The Premise
A ragged underhive thief, Shulie, attempts a pickpocket on the wrong mark and gets drafted into an undercover Inquisitorial cell deep in the Necromundan Underhive. The cell has lost a member and needs a native guide to reach a "profane" target.

## 2. The Setting: Necromunda Underhive
*   **Atmosphere:** Oppressive, claustrophobic, toxic, and utterly ruthless. The air tastes of promethium and old blood.
*   **Key Elements:** Sump-lakes, crumbling mega-structures, forgotten archeotech, and sprawling gang territories. 

## 3. The Patron
*   **Faction:** The Inquisition
*   **Duty:** Ordo Hereticus Inquisitor — hunts heresy, rogue psykers, and unsanctioned cults
*   **Motivation:** Information — collects intelligence to root out threats before they metastasize
*   **Demeanour:** Inscrutable — conceals intentions behind cold professionalism; the cell never knows their full plan
*   **Boon — Limitless Authority:** The Inquisitor's rosette can override any local Imperial authority — Arbites, Planetary Governors, even Mechanicus installations — but invoking it reveals the cell's presence and burns the operation's cover
*   **Boon — Sanctum Obscurus:** A hidden safehouse somewhere above the Underhive stocked with surveillance gear and coded vox channels, available only if the cell can reach it
*   **Liability — Demands Discretion:** The Inquisitor insists on absolute secrecy. Using the rosette or revealing the cell's Inquisitorial ties means severe punishment — or disappearance

## 4. The Party
*   **Shulie (Player Character):** Underhive scum, 20-ish, latent Wyrd. Has a stubborn streak, Granny's six-stubber, and an unsettling "bad luck charm". They are surviving day-by-day.
*   **The Interrogator (NPC):** The ruthless, bolt-pistol-wielding leader of the cell. Pragmatic, views Shulie as an expendable asset but values their local knowledge. 
*   **"The Bald One" (NPC):** An unnerving member of the cell who gives Shulie the "side-eye." Potentially an unsanctioned psyker, a blank, or just a paranoid veteran who senses Shulie's latent "Wyrd" nature.
*   **The Deceased (Missing NPC):** The cell member Shulie is replacing. How did they die? What did they carry that Shulie now has to manage?

## 5. The Opposition
*   **The "Profane" Target:** The primary objective of the Inquisitorial cell. This could be a Heretek cult tinkering with forbidden archaeotech, a Genestealer staging ground, or a Chaos coven. The exact nature should be revealed slowly.
*   **The Underhive Ecology:** Gangers (Goliath, Escher, Delaque, etc.), mutated sumpkrocs, scavvies, and the hazardous environment itself (toxic gas pockets, collapsing domes).
*   **The Inquisitorial Cell (Internal Threat):** If Shulie's Wyrd powers manifest too obviously, the Interrogator will likely execute them on the spot as an unsanctioned witch.

## 6. Themes & Tone
*   **Survival Horror & Paranoia:** Shulie is caught between the horrors of the Underhive and the uncompromising dogmatism of the Inquisition. 
*   **Latent Awakening:** Shulie's psychic powers ("Granny's Luck") will manifest under stress, bringing both salvation and the terrible danger of Warp tracking.
*   **Class/Power disparity:** The offworlders have superior gear and absolute authority, but they are helpless without Shulie's street smarts to navigate the locals and the terrain.

## 7. Flexible Structure
*   **Macro-Structure (The Rails):** The Interrogator dictates the ultimate goal (e.g., "Take us to the collapsed Dome of St. Sabbatine").
*   **Micro-Structure (The Sandbox):** Shulie dictates *how* they get there, choosing the paths, deciding which gangs to parley with, and avoiding known hazards.
*   **Branching Elements:**
    *   Shulie runs into their old gang - how do they explain their new "friends"?
    *   Granny's "Bad Luck Charm" begins whispering instructions in Shulie's mind.
    *   The Inquisitorial cell gets ambushed and separated.
